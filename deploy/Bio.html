Kristina is a Los Angeles-based photographer specializing in portraiture of performing artists.
Her inspiration is drawn from her background as a classical pianist and the vibrant but
isolating nature of urban culture.

Kristina, the daughter of a South Korean mother and a Michigan-born father, studied piano performance at USC.
Realizing that a performance career wasn't exactly for her, she transitioned into her other passion: photography.

Her first camera was her father's Olympus OM-1 with a broken light meter (which she lost in a cab in Mexico),
but her love for photography began in her high school's darkroom. To this day, a huge part of her practice is in the digital darkroom,
although it only heightens, but does not create, the photographs.

A contributor to the indie-classical blog, reclassical, Kristina also teaches piano and plays Chopin, Debussy, Beethoven, etcetera on her bluesy upright.
